Electronic Staff Record (ESR)

What is ESR?

The Electronic Staff Record (ESR) is a human resources and payroll system used by most NHS Trusts in England and Wales. It manages the payroll for over one million NHS employees.

About ESR Self Service

ESR Self Service gives you direct access to manage and view your workforce information. There are three levels of access:

  • Employee Self Service (already available to all colleagues): View and update personal information such as address, contact details, and equality data. Access payslips and e-learning.
  • Supervisor Self Service (Available from 1 September 2025): Line managers can view and manage information for their direct reports, including employment details, training compliance, and appraisal dates. No pay-impacting changes allowed. Assigned to line managers who are not authorised signatories.
  • Manager Self Service (Available from 1 September 2025): Includes Supervisor access plus ability to make pay-impacting changes (e.g., amend working hours, submit leaver notifications). Assigned to line managers who are authorised signatories.
Accessing ESR

If you have Manager or Supervisor ESR access, you’ll need a smartcard to log in. Click here for smartcard login instructions.

If you have Employee ESR access only, you can log in using your username and password.

For login issues or to request Manager/Supervisor access, please contact the ESR Self Service helpdesk (details below).

FAQs

Can I only access current payslips?

Both current and historical payslips are available. Historical records go back to your start date or 2014.

Will I be able to access my e-payslips after leaving the Trust?

Yes, via the ESR Leavers Dashboard for 90 days post-departure. Be sure to download any documents you wish to keep.

Is ESR Self Service the same system used for e-learning?

Yes. ESR is the Trust’s HR, Payroll, and Learning Management system.

What if I forget my password?

Use the “Forgotten password” link on the ESR login screen. For login issues, contact scn-tr.esr_self_service@nhs.net

Can I access ESR on my phone or tablet?

Yes, but first enable internet access from an NHS network (N3). Use the button in the top right of the ESR Portal homepage. Once granted, you can access ESR remotely or via the MyESR app.

How do I access the My ESR App?

Download it from the Apple or Android app stores. Search “My ESR” to locate.

Can I use a smartcard for Employee Self Service?

Yes, although it’s not required. You can log in with your username and password.

ESR Manager Self Service

Introduction

ESR Manager Self Service enables managers to update and action changes for their team in ESR.

There are two levels of access:

  • Supervisor Self Service: View and manage direct reports’ information (employment, training, appraisals). No pay-impacting changes. Assigned to line managers who are not authorised signatories.
  • Manager Self Service: Includes Supervisor access plus pay-impacting changes (e.g. working hours, leaver notifications). Assigned to authorised signatories.

Below you’ll find links to access ESR, supported changes, user guides, and helpdesk contact details.

Accessing Manager/Supervisor Self Service

FAQs

Do I need a smartcard to log in?

Yes. Username/password login is not supported for Manager Self Service. Contact the helpdesk if needed.

Why am I getting a future dated change error?

There’s already a future change in the system. You can’t select an earlier effective date. Contact the helpdesk for support.

How do I make changes for someone with multiple assignments?

Double-check assignment details and collaborate with other managers if needed. Ensure correct assignment is selected.

I’ve just become a manager—how do I get access?

Contact the ESR Self Service Team to request access.

What’s the difference between Supervisor and Additional Supervisor?

Supervisors are line managers. Additional Supervisors are added when another colleague needs access (e.g. for appraisals). Avoid adding senior managers already in the hierarchy to prevent loop errors.
